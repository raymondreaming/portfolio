Rthmn starts from a simple premise: market data becomes more useful when structure is visible. Price, time, liquidity, volatility, and participation all carry shape. The interface should make those relationships easier to see, compare, and act on without forcing the trader to decode raw noise.

The work sits between trading infrastructure and visual design. I think about data pipelines, latency, rendering performance, and signal quality, but I also think about rhythm, contrast, hierarchy, and pattern recognition. A trading tool has to be fast, but speed only matters when the resulting view helps a human or algorithm make a better decision.

The goal is a platform where complex financial data becomes geometric language: real-time patterns, spatial relationships, and algorithmic signals that can be studied, tested, and eventually automated.
