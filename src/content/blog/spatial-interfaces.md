Spatial software changes the way an interface feels. Instead of treating every screen as a flat document, 3D systems let information have depth, distance, motion, and presence. That matters when the subject is complex: market structure, creative systems, learning environments, or tools for exploring perception.

I use 3D and motion when they clarify relationships, not as decoration. The best spatial interfaces help the user build a mental model faster. They make state visible, reduce ambiguity, and give information a body that can be inspected from more than one angle.

Reality Designers is where this overlaps with creative systems: perception, consciousness, human potential, and the design of environments that shape how people think.
